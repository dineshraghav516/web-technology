BUG HUNTING

Bug hunting, in the context of cybersecurity, refers to the process of finding and reporting security vulnerabilities in software and systems. It's a proactive approach where individuals, often called ethical hackers or penetration testers, systematically search for weaknesses that could be exploited by malicious actors. This practice helps improve the security of systems by identifying and addressing vulnerabilities before they can be exploited by attackers.

ETHICAL HACKING:-

Ethical hacking is the practice of legally accessing computer systems, networks, or applications with the permission of the owner to identify security vulnerabilities. It involves using the same techniques as malicious hackers, but with the goal of improving security rather than causing harm. Ethical hackers, also known as white hat hackers, help organizations identify weaknesses and strengthen their defenses against cyberattacks.
